1. aunt (proposed definition)

The term aunt looks similar to other terms in the kinship database (aunt in English, aunt in English). In these languages, the term is defined as follows:

parent's sister or parent's brother's wife or mother's mother's father's child's child's test female or father's mother's father's child's child's test female or mother's mother's father's child's child's test male wife or father's mother's father's child's child's test male wife

Consider each of the following questions in turn:

  1. Can you think of a relationship that matches "parent's sister", but is not called aunt?
  2. Can you think of a relationship that matches "parent's brother's wife", but is not called aunt?
  3. Can you think of a relationship that matches "mother's mother's father's child's child's test female", but is not called aunt?
  4. Can you think of a relationship that matches "father's mother's father's child's child's test female", but is not called aunt?
  5. Can you think of a relationship that matches "mother's mother's father's child's child's test male wife", but is not called aunt?
  6. Can you think of a relationship that matches "father's mother's father's child's child's test male wife", but is not called aunt?
  7. Are there any other relationships called aunt that are not covered by the above?

Options:

  • If your answer to all of these questions is "No", then Accept the proposed definition.
  • For every "Yes" answer, go to the genealogy editor and add examples of the relationships that do not fit the proposed definition. The proposed definition will go away the next time you "Get New Suggestions". You may also specify Reject now in order to remove this item from the list of pending suggestions. Note, however, if you simply Reject without entering any counterevidence, SILKin will never repeat this suggestion -- but you are not helping it find a better one the next time you "Get New Suggestions".
  • If you are not sure, you can do nothing for now.
